Jake Vanderplas

I am a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Washington, working with Andrew Connolly in the Astronomy Department’s Survey Science Group. In January, I will begin an NSF post-doctoral fellowship under Magdalena Balazinska in the Database Research Group in UW Computer Science and Engineering.

Astronomy Research

On the astronomy side, I’m interested in data-intensive cosmological studies, particularly in the area of weak gravitational lensing. My PhD thesis focused on Karhunen-Loeve analysis and its applications to weak lensing science. You can see a current list of my astronomy-related publications on ADS.

Python

I am actively involved in the python open source community as a core contributor to both scikit-learn and scipy. See my github profile: jakevdp.

Education and Outreach

Before embarking on my PhD, I spent several years working as an outdoor science educator at the Mount Hermon Outdoor Science School near Santa Cruz, CA. I still enjoy engaging with the public about science, primarily as a volunteer at the University of Washington Planetarium, as a Science Communication Fellow at Seattle’s Pacific Science Center, and through various public forums around Seattle.
